Iekei Souhonzan Ramen Yoshimuraya

(RamenDB) Yokohama or Hiranumabashi

Progenitor of iekei style ramen, a cult-favorite style of ramen that canonically features pork and chicken soup, chicken oil, smoked chashu, a generous variety of tableside seasonings, and is generally eaten with rice.

(RamenDB) Imaike

Local chain in Nagoya that pioneered Taiwan ramen, a distant descendant of the Taiwanese danzimian. Soup is punchy, meaty, and very spicy.

Trendy shop in Osaka. Three main styles of ramen served: a genten that represents a take on old school shoyu ramen, a micro that represents a more refined take on the local Takaida style ramen, and a clam-based macro.
